Special Moves

The game of chess has a few special moves that can be played in certain circumstances. This section will outline them.

En Passant

If a pawn moves two squares forward to end up aligned horizontally with a pawn of opposite colour, the opposite coloured pawn can capture the other pawn as if it only moved one square forward. This move is called “en passant” (a French expression meaning “in passing”) and can only be played on the move immediately after the pawn is pushed two squares.

Castling

Castling is a special king move that can be played when both the king and a rook have not been moved yet in the game. To play this move, the king moves two squares horizontally toward a rook that has not yet been moved and the rook then swings around to the square on the opposite side of the king. A player cannot castle into check nor castle through check.
